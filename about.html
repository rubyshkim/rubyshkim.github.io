ABOUT ME

I am a fourth year PhD student at Duke University interested in mathematical biology and dynamical systems. I am currently working with Dr. Michael Reed on the mathematical modeling of circadian rhythms, and on modeling sex differences in one-carbon metabolism. I am also interested in bifurcation theory and numerical analysis.

I was an Instructor of Record for Math 105L during Fall 2018 and Fall 2019, and for Math 106L during Spring 2020. In Spring 2019, I started the Duke chapter of the Association for Women in Mathematics (AWM). I am also currently the president of the Duke chapter of the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM).
