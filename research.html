Circadian Rhythms and Dopamine

I use mathematics to study the reciprocal interactions between the circadian clock and the dopaminergic system.

Analysis

I am using ideas from bifurcation theory and numerical analysis to study the dynamics of coupled circadian oscillators. I am investigating the simultaneous robustness and flexibility of the circadian clock circuitry.

Mathematics Community and Education

Mathematics is not apolitical. As a mathematician and educator, I am responsible for learning about the impacts of my research and teaching on people's lives. I have been learning about the ways in which our field unjustly upholds and contributes to the systemic exclusion of women and Black and Indigeneous People of Color (BIPOC). Below is a petition written to the Duke University Mathematics Department on August 3rd, 2020. This document lists suggestions for ways we can begin taking a stance against instiutionalized racism. Also included are references that explain the histories of institutional barriers and call for the need to foster diversity, equity, and inclusion in the mathematics community.
